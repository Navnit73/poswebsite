INDEPENDENT PHARMACY · PUNE

City Pharmacy cut expiry write-offs by 62% in 90 days.

City Pharmacy is a single-counter retail pharmacy in Kothrud, Pune, serving 120–150 customers per day. Before Formulary, they tracked batches on paper and frequently discovered expired stock during quarterly counts.

After switching to Formulary, FEFO billing automatically prioritised the earliest expiring batch. The 30-day expiry alert on the dashboard gave the pharmacist enough time to push near-expiry stock with discounts or return it to the distributor.

"We stopped writing expiry dates on a whiteboard the week we switched. The register just won't sell the wrong batch anymore."

— Store Manager, City Pharmacy

SMALL CHAIN · BANGALORE

Wellness Rx unified 3 stores under one dashboard.

Wellness Rx operates three outlets across Bangalore. Each store had its own spreadsheet, its own product naming conventions, and its own way of counting stock. Transfer between stores meant a phone call and a hope.

On Formulary's Growth plan, all three stores share one product catalogue with per-store inventory. The owner checks a consolidated dashboard every morning, with low-stock and expiry alerts filtered by store.

"For the first time I can see all three stores on one screen. No more calling the Jayanagar store to ask how much Metformin they have."

— Owner, Wellness Rx

RURAL CLINIC PHARMACY · SATARA

A 200-SKU pharmacy went from a notebook to a live dashboard.

Dr. Joshi runs a small clinic with an attached dispensary in rural Satara. With 200 SKUs and one assistant, the "inventory system" was a ruled notebook and a calculator. Stock counts happened when something ran out.

Formulary's Starter plan gave the clinic batch-level tracking, automatic low-stock alerts, and a daily email summary — all for ₹3,200 per month. The assistant learned the POS terminal in half a day.

"I used to find expired stock behind other boxes. Now the system tells me 30 days before anything expires. That alone pays for itself."

— Dr. Joshi, Clinic Owner
